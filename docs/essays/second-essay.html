Beauty and Stillness

“If, by contrast, we have surrendered to the enjoyment of genuine beauty, we are at such a moment in equal degree of our passive and active powers...” (Schiller, Letters on the Aesthetic Education of Man, 149)

I observed a tree in the Cambridge Commons while considering the sentence...

I allowed my focus on the tree to be briefly interrupted by the noticing of the confused expressions of passersby...

I had been wondering what Schiller would think about the modern person’s typical forms of aesthetic engagement...

In order to truly encounter beauty, you need to be unafraid to sit in stillness with it, even if that means you must sit with yourself as well.
